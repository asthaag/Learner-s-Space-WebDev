Game Overview

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on a checkered board with 64 squares arranged in an 8Ã—8 grid. The game is believed to have originated in India around the 7th century. The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king by putting it in check and making it impossible for them to escape!

Game Highlights

Notable chess matches include the 1972 World Championship between Bobby Fischer and Boris Spassky, and famous players such as Garry Kasparov, Magnus Carlsen, and Judit Polgar.

Know About The Pieces

A chess set has 32 pieces, also called chessmen, in total, with each player receiving 16 pieces in either dark or light colors.
The six types of chess pieces are: Queen, King, Rook, Knight, Pawn, Bishop

How to Get Started

To start playing chess, you'll need a chessboard and a set of pieces. Basic strategies include controlling the center, developing your pieces, and protecting your king.

  • Equipment: Chessboard and pieces
  • Basic Strategies: Control the center, develop pieces, protect the king
  • Beginner Tips: Practice regularly, study classic games, solve puzzles
